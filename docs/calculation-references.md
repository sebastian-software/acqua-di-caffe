# Coffee-Water Calculation and References

Last reviewed: 2026-05-06

## Formulas

The calculator formulas stay close to the Kaffeemacher guide because they work directly with the mineral values printed on bottled-water labels:

- `Total hardness (dGH) = calciumMgL / 7.1 + magnesiumMgL / 4.35`
- `Alkalinity (d Alk) = bicarbonateMgL / 21.8`

Kaffeemacher demonstrates this calculation with Volvic: calcium `12 mg/L`, magnesium `8 mg/L`, and bicarbonate `74 mg/L` result in roughly `3.53 dGH` and `3.39 d Alk`.

Source: [Kaffeemacher coffee-water guide](https://kaffeemacher.de/blogs/kaffeewissen/kaffeewasser)

## App Target Ranges

The app no longer evaluates water only against Kaffeemacher's very narrow filter-coffee range. Instead, it uses three zones per beverage:

- `Core range`: a strict range that can be derived from the tighter references.
- `Extended range`: a practical range supported by SCA/SCAE, German specialty-coffee sources, and espresso-machine specifications.
- `Usable range`: water that is not ideal, but should not look catastrophic in the app. Very close misses may still receive grade 2; most water in this zone receives grade 3.

| Target        | Core GH   | Core Alk    | Extended GH  | Extended Alk      | Usable GH   | Usable Alk     | Rationale                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        |
| ------------- | --------: | ----------: | -----------: | ----------------: | ----------: | -------------: | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Filter coffee | `2-3 dGH` | `1-2 d Alk` | `1.5-8 dGH`  | `0.8-4.5 d Alk`   | `0.5-12 dGH` | `0.5-9 d Alk`  | Keeps the narrow Kaffeemacher, Earlybird, and Roastmarket filter range as the strict core while avoiding unnecessary penalties for SCA/SCAE-compatible water.                                                                                                    |
| Espresso      | `3-6 dGH` | `2-4 d Alk` | `2-7.5 dGH`  | `1.5-4.8 d Alk`   | `1-12 dGH`   | `0.8-9 d Alk`  | Kaffeemacher, Coffee Circle, Roastmarket, La Marzocco, and Portioli support a higher practical espresso range. The generous alkalinity tolerance is intentionally only a tolerance zone, not an ideal or general recommendation.                                  |

Water inside the core range receives grade 1. Water inside the extended range is rated as good, but not as a core hit. Water inside the usable range is treated as acceptable. Water outside these ranges is still graded gradually. A small miss therefore becomes grade 2 or 3 rather than "unsuitable."

## Why Filter Coffee Is Rated More Broadly

The old app logic treated `2-3 dGH` and `1-2 d Alk` as the only suitable filter-coffee range. This is defensible as a narrow ideal from Kaffeemacher, but it is too strict compared with SCA/Water for Coffee. The three-zone model keeps this range visible as the core, while `1.5-8 dGH` and `0.8-4.5 d Alk` act as an extended range that does not unnecessarily penalize realistically useful bottled waters.

The additional sources checked on 2026-04-30 support this direction: Vector and 19grams support the broader SCA range, Earlybird supports the narrow filter ideal, and Kaffee-Netz practice discussions show that many users get good results outside the tight `2-3 dGH` window or deliberately mix waters.

## Why Espresso Tolerates More Alkalinity

The source review from 2026-05-06 supports the direction that espresso can tolerate more buffered water than filter coffee. The most important reason is beverage ratio. Espresso uses much less water for the same amount of coffee than filter coffee; therefore, less alkalinity is available to buffer acids from the coffee. Marco Wellinger's SCA/ZHAW explanation describes this relationship directly.

For the app, this does not mean that "more is always better." La Marzocco, Sanremo, and Portioli show that espresso machines and espresso sensory quality still need clear boundaries. Therefore, `3-6 dGH` and `2-4 d Alk` remain the core range. The extended range is raised to `2-7.5 dGH` and `1.5-4.8 d Alk` because that aligns well with machine and practice sources. Even higher alkalinity is shown only as a tolerance range and should not be read as a general recommendation for every machine.
